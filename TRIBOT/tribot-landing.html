What TRIBOT is

Not a rulebook. A learner.

Generic policy engines check every call against the same static list. TRIBOT spends seven days learning your specific agent's rolling behavioral baseline — tool, args, sequence — and then flags drift. An agent that suddenly calls delete_file at 3am is a signal, even if "delete_file" was technically allowed.

Three independent angles, not one.

Every tool call is judged simultaneously by Persona (in-character?), Skills (capability allowed?), and Runtime (destination safe?). A single-policy proxy can't see what three independent eyes see.

Master never acts alone.

Three verdicts arrive at the Master. It synthesizes, but never autonomously executes a contested call. When the supervisors disagree, Master surfaces the conflict to you. The kill switch was never the agent's; it always belonged to the human at the keyboard.

Your agent's baseline is yours alone.

Seven days of rolling histograms (tool × args-shape × sequence) build a fingerprint that lives only on your machine. Drift detection compares your agent against itself — not against a generic OWASP rule.

One hundred percent local.

No cloud control plane. No telemetry. SQLCipher with the key in your OS keyring. If the company disappears tomorrow, your installation keeps running, your audit chain stays verifiable offline, and your data never left your laptop.

Hash-chained audit, byte-level tamper detection.

Every verdict and decision is appended to an HMAC-SHA256 chain. Flip one byte in the past and tribot verify-chain fails at the exact row — offline, on your machine. You will know.

~280ms latency budget, end-to-end.

Soul 50ms + Skills 10ms + Connection 200ms + Master 20ms. Comfortably inside Nielsen's 1-second flow threshold — with room for a Haiku tie-breaker on contested calls.

Defends against the realistic threats, names them.

Prompt injection via pasted or fetched content. Memory poisoning. Malicious MCP installs. Local data theft. Outbound exfiltration. We don't claim SOC2 or nation-state coverage — we ship what you can verify.

One-click install. Built for non-developers.

Microsoft AGT and Veto.so are SDKs — engineers must wire them in. TRIBOT is a Tauri-signed desktop app. Double-click, point at your agent, pick a Persona. Five minutes from download to baseline-learning mode.

Questions

Frequently asked.

How is this different from Microsoft AGT, Veto.so, or a 200-line Python proxy?
Those are SDKs for engineers. TRIBOT is a desktop app — a non-developer installs it in one click. And rules engines check every call against a generic policy; TRIBOT learns your specific agent's baseline over 7 days and flags drift — an agent that suddenly calls delete_file at 3am is a signal, even if "delete_file" was technically allowed. 100% local, no telemetry, no cloud control plane. Free tier that actually protects; $19/mo solo when v1 ships.
What does "three independent supervisors" actually buy me over one good policy engine?
Three uncorrelated failure modes. Persona uses an LLM-judge against a manifest (PSG-Agent). Skills uses statistical baseline deviation (UEBA). Runtime uses a DeBERTa prompt-injection classifier (Lakera PINT 97.7%). Each is blind to what the others see — so a payload that fools one rarely fools all three. Master denies on disagreement by default; you decide on contested calls. A single-policy SDK can't do this without becoming three projects.
Does Master work with Claude Desktop, Cursor, or other MCP runtimes?
Architecture is MCP-runtime-agnostic — the four verbs (intercept, inspect, gate, record) don't care which agent speaks MCP. At launch we support the Claude Agent SDK; additional runtimes will follow. Closed-source runtimes where we can't sit on the tool-call boundary are not supported — the security model is incoherent without that access.
Can I override Master when it blocks something?
Yes — the kill switch works both ways. Every block surfaces the three verdicts from Persona, Skills, and Runtime and gives you three choices: allow this once, update the rule permanently, or cancel the run. Master surfaces; you decide. The final word on every tool call is always yours — that's the point of the whole architecture.
You're a solo founder. Why bet production workflows on you?
Fair. That's why TRIBOT is 100% local with no cloud control plane — if I disappear tomorrow, your installation keeps running, your audit chain stays verifiable offline, and your data never left your machine. Everything exports as open-schema JSON. Skills are portable OCI artifacts. No vendor lock-in because there's no vendor infrastructure to lock you into.
What does TRIBOT defend against, honestly?
Prompt injection via pasted or fetched content. Memory poisoning. Malicious MCP installs. Local data theft. Outbound exfiltration. We do not claim 3rd-party audit, SOC2, ISO, EU DPA, source-code escrow, nation-state coverage, or cloud-provider retention guarantees. We ship what we can verify — and the audit chain proves it.
How does this play with GDPR Article 22 (automated decisions)?
Master operates a two-lane architecture. Safety-critical blocks (malware, CSAM, sanctioned endpoints) are automatic under Art. 22(2). Discretionary blocks route to a human review queue with 24h SLA — satisfying Art. 22(3) and AI Act Art. 14 human-oversight duties. Appeal path is published in the ToS; DPA is pre-signed and linked on this page.
